About this map. Places are drawn from the events in the companion timeline; each marker's popup links back to the matching event. Ancient sites are shown at their modern locations (Edessa = Şanlıurfa, Nisibis = Nusaybin, Seleucia-Ctesiphon near Baghdad, Mylapore in Chennai). Kerala places pinpoint towns named in the record — Kodungallur, Kochi, Kottayam, Mulanthuruthy, Thozhiyur, Niranam, and others. A dashed line traces the eastward spread of Syriac Christianity along the Roman–Persian land routes and the Arabian-Sea spice lane to India; East Syriac Christianity also reached Central Asia and Tang China (the Xi'an stele, 781), beyond this frame. A few far-flung events aren't mapped — such as King Alfred's 9th-century English embassy to India. Base map © OpenStreetMap contributors, tiles © CARTO.
